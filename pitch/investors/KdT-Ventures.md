**Subject: Provenika — an auditable evidence engine for oncology discovery ("compute or cite, never assert")**

Cain — KdT backs founders rewriting the physical layer, and treats computation, simulation, and AI as the instruments that let us read and redesign biology and chemistry rigorously. Provenika is built for exactly that worldview: the discipline isn't the model, it's the **provenance**.

**What it is.** Provenika (open-source, provenika.com) is an *auditable evidence engine* for oncology drug-discovery research. It turns free public data — ChEMBL, UniProt, PDB, PubMed, ClinicalTrials.gov — into ranked, fully-cited, independently re-verifiable research hypotheses: target triage, ligand shortlists, structures, docking setup, and feasibility. The operating rule is **"compute or cite, never assert."** Every number is either deterministically computed or fetched-and-cited.

**The wedge a physician-scientist can trust.** Incumbents — Open Targets, Schrödinger, Insilico, Causaly, BenchSci — ship *closed, black-box* outputs you cannot audit. Provenika ships the opposite: a one-command verifier re-pulls each figure from its public source and returns **PASS / DRIFT / FAIL**. As a pathologist who studied non-coding RNA in cancer at the NCI, you know the bottleneck isn't generating hypotheses — it's deciding which to trust. Re-verifiable provenance is that trust, made mechanical. The repo already ships real CADD: cheminformatics precompute across 18 oncology targets (ligand efficiency, chemotype clustering).

**Honest weaknesses I won't hide.** All sources are public — no data moat; the moat is the auditable methodology and the verifier. Open Targets gives the headline triage away free; our differentiator is provenance, not coverage. And to be precise about category: this is **research tooling, not a cure, drug, diagnostic, or medical advice** — no patient-facing claims.
