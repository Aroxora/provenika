# Targets for China's highest-burden cancers

Curing cancer in China starts with the cancers that take the most lives there. This crosses the genetics-ranked target panel with China's national cancer burden — surfacing genetically-validated targets that map onto a top Chinese cancer.

## China's cancer burden — which cancers to work on

China, 2022: **~4.82 million** new cancer cases and **~2.57 million** cancer deaths. The five leading causes of cancer **death** — lung, liver, stomach, colorectum, oesophagus — are **67.5%** of all cancer deaths. Pursuing genetically-validated targets *for these cancers* is where targeted-therapy work matters most for Chinese patients.

| Cancer | 中文 | China death rank | 2022 new cases | Dominant driver | Highest-impact prevention lever |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lung cancer | 肺癌 | #1 | 1,060,600 | tobacco + ambient/household air pollution | tobacco control / smoking cessation (the dominant modifiable risk) |
| Liver cancer (HCC) | 肝癌 | #2 | 367,700 | chronic hepatitis B (HBV); aflatoxin, alcohol, NAFLD | HBV vaccination — prevents the chronic infection behind most Chinese HCC (the first 'anti-cancer vaccine'; WHO/IARC); plus antiviral treatment of chronic HBV |
| Stomach (gastric) cancer | 胃癌 | #3 | 358,700 | Helicobacter pylori; diet (salt, preserved foods) | H. pylori test-and-treat — eradicating an IARC class-1 carcinogen lowers gastric-cancer risk (Choi, NEJM 2020; Cochrane) |
| Colorectal cancer | 结直肠癌 | #4 | 517,100 | diet/lifestyle, obesity; incidence rising in China | screening (colonoscopy / FIT) with adenoma removal — interrupts the adenoma→carcinoma sequence |
| Oesophageal cancer | 食管癌 | #5 | — | hot beverages, alcohol/tobacco, diet; regional 'cancer belts' | avoid very-hot beverages, tobacco and heavy alcohol; address the regional risk factors |
| Breast cancer | 乳腺癌 | — (high incidence) | — | the leading cancer in Chinese women by incidence | screening (mammography) for early detection |

> **Honest read-out:** for **Liver cancer (HCC), Oesophageal cancer** the proven, highest-impact lever in China is **prevention** (HBV vaccination; reducing exposures), not a new small molecule — these cancers are hard to drug. A targeted-therapy hypothesis here is the complement, not the headline. Lung, colorectal, gastric and breast carry genuine small-molecule target biology, which is where this pipeline contributes most.

_Source: Han B, Zeng H, Chen W, et al. Cancer incidence and mortality in China, 2022. J Natl Cancer Center 2024 (PMID 39036382; National Cancer Center of China). <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39036382/>. National 2022 estimates, not per-program forecasts; target↔cancer mapping is a relevance heuristic. Research only; not medical advice._
